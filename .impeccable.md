## Design Context

### Users
Two audiences that must both be served:
- **Recruiters / HR**: Fast scanners — first 5 seconds matter. Need immediate credibility signals: title, role clarity, visual quality that says "this person ships real things".
- **Hiring managers / eng leads**: Technical depth-seekers — they'll scroll, expand timeline cards, read project descriptions. Need evidence of craft, thoughtful engineering, and signal-to-noise ratio.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp & technical.** Precise, confident, engineered. The site should feel like a tool built by someone who cares deeply about craft — not a template someone filled in. Three words: **Precise. Purposeful. Distinctive.**

Emotional goals:
- Recruiter: "This is someone serious"
- Eng lead: "This person sweats the details"

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Light and dark modes** both supported, equally polished
- **Anti-reference**: Anything that looks like it was generated by AI or scaffolded from a template — glassmorphism everywhere, identical icon+label cards, gradient text for "impact", purple-to-blue gradients on dark, generic rounded cards with drop shadows
- **Primary weakness to address**: Too generic/safe — needs distinctive personality, not just functional polish
- **Typography**: Bricolage Grotesque (headings) + Inter (body) — already good, lean into the Bricolage weight range more aggressively
- **Color**: Purple accent (~259°) — use it with conviction, not just as decoration
- **Motion**: Lenis + Framer Motion already in place — use motion to convey craft, not fill space

### Design Principles
1. **Engineer's taste, not designer's flair** — every detail serves a purpose; decoration is earned
2. **Signal over noise** — ruthless about what earns space on the page; each element should pull weight
3. **Consistent rhythm** — spacing, sizing, and type hierarchy should feel systematic, not eyeballed
4. **States are first-class** — every interactive surface has hover, focus, and active states that feel considered
5. **Both audiences in one breath** — the recruiter reads the hero and knows; the eng lead digs deeper and is rewarded
